# style-korean-minhwa

## Prompt
"A master-level, authentic Korean Folk Art (Minhwa) portrait of the subject pet from the uploaded image. Strictly exclude all modern studio equipment, easels, and western artist's tools. The background is a soft, warm-toned aged mulberry paper (Hanji) texture, featuring subtle traditional motifs like stylized pine branches or clouds in the far corners, fading into a seamless antique void.

All attention is focused on the pet, rendered with the elegant, rhythmic ink-and-wash lines characteristic of the Joseon dynasty. The pet is seated in a dignified yet whimsical 'Hogwon' pose, looking directly at the viewer with eyes that capture its specific shape and soul. The fur is not rendered photo-realistically but through delicate, repetitive brush-strokes that emphasize its pattern and texture on the paper.

The color palette is composed of traditional 'Obangsaek' (five cardinal colors), using muted, earthy pigments like indigo, deep red, and ochre. The lighting is flat yet warm, consistent with traditional oriental painting styles, focusing on the clarity of the subject's form. The entire image has the visible, fibrous texture of hand-pressed Hanji paper and the soulful, humorous charm of a centuries-old Korean masterpiece."
